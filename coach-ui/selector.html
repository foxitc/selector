The fairness problem: A mum working Mon–Thu lunches and a weekend evening clerk are doing fundamentally different jobs. Comparing raw totals is unfair. Service bands let you set a target multiplier per service type — for example, a lunch service might have a spend target of 70% of an evening service. The Selector automatically assigns each transaction to its service band and benchmarks accordingly.

Benchmarking Approach
✓ ACTIVE — Hybrid Model
Fixed floors + Peer comparison
Minimum standards everyone must meet, plus dynamic benchmarking against peers in the same service band. Most defensible — you can explain every score.
Option B
Dynamic peer benchmarking only
Targets auto-calculated from 4-week rolling average per service band. Self-calibrating but needs several weeks of data first.
Option A
Fixed targets with band multipliers
Jack sets one target per metric, system applies a multiplier per service band. Simple and transparent.
Pub vs Pub Fairness
Within-venue scoring (Coaching)
Each team member is measured against peers at their own venue. A Griffin Inn clerk competes against other Griffin Inn clerks. This is what the Coach shows GMs — it's the most useful for day-to-day coaching conversations.
Used for individual coaching
Group-wide scoring (League Table)
Cross-venue comparison normalised by venue averages. A score of 75 at Long Hop means the same as a 75 at Tap & Run — both are performing at the same level relative to their venue baseline. Used for the overall Test Player league table.
